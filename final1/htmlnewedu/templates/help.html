Key Risk Factors

High Impact Factors

  • • Attendance below 75%
  • • Failing grades (D/F)
  • • Low total scores (< 50)
  • • Minimal study hours (< 5/week)

Medium Impact Factors

  • • Attendance 75-85%
  • • C grades
  • • Moderate study hours (5-8/week)
  • • Low project scores

Positive Indicators

  • • High attendance (> 85%)
  • • A/B grades
  • • Strong project scores
  • • Consistent study habits

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How is a student marked as "At-Risk"?

A student is classified as "At-Risk" based on multiple factors:

  • Attendance below 75%
  • Total score less than 50 points
  • Grades of D or F
  • Risk score above 0.7 (70%)
  • Study hours less than 5 per week

Our AI model analyzes these factors along with historical data to provide accurate risk assessments.

Can I edit student data in the system?

Currently, the system is designed for viewing and analyzing student data. Data modifications should be made in your primary student information system, and the data will be updated in EduRisk AI during the next sync cycle. This ensures data integrity and prevents conflicts.

How often is the risk assessment updated?

Risk assessments are updated in real-time as new data becomes available. The system processes:

  • Daily attendance updates
  • Weekly grade submissions
  • Monthly performance reviews
  • Quarterly comprehensive assessments
What should I do when I identify an at-risk student?

When you identify an at-risk student, follow these steps:

  1. Review Details: Click on the student's record to view their complete profile and performance analysis.
  2. Contact Student: Reach out to discuss their challenges and offer support.
  3. Academic Support: Arrange tutoring, study groups, or additional resources.
  4. Regular Monitoring: Track their progress weekly and adjust interventions as needed.
  5. Document Actions: Keep records of interventions and outcomes for future reference.
How accurate is the risk prediction model?

Our risk prediction model has been trained on extensive educational data and achieves approximately 85% accuracy. The model considers multiple factors including attendance patterns, academic performance, study habits, and historical trends. However, it's important to use this as a tool to guide interventions rather than make final decisions, as individual circumstances may vary.

Can I export reports for administrative purposes?

Yes! You can export data in several ways:

  • Student Data: Use the "Export CSV" button on the Students page to download filtered student records.
  • Reports: Generate comprehensive reports from the Reports page for administrative review.
  • Custom Filters: Apply specific filters before exporting to get targeted data sets.
  • Regular Updates: Set up automated reports for weekly or monthly administrative reviews.
